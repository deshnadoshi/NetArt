404 Not Found!

Curated by Deshna Doshi

This exhibition is a curation of various net art projects that all reflect the relationship between human beings and the internet that separates them. These works are examples of how the internet has begun imitating human experience, bringing anonymity, intimacy, and collective memory together through different forms of data collection. Lovelines and We Feel Fine by Jonathan Harris and Sep Kamvar highlight the importance of emotional expression, using data found through online blogs to capture the range of human emotions. The Unsent Project by Rora Blue also delves into the words left unsaid in modern communication by exploring the vulnerability and complexity of unexpressed emotions. These websites all explore the depth of human connection in the digital age, creating large repositories of emotional experiences that in another time might have remained private or personal. Net art of this type almost creates an avenue for emotional release or expression. What was a quiet, personal catharsis of love, hate, regret, etc. is now made public, for the world to see. These projects emphasize that the nature of the internet is paradoxical. The internet is a space, where despite its vastness, people are able to form meaningful connections and express even the most obscure human feelings.


Lovelines (2006) by Jonathan Harris and Sep Kamvar
Lovelines collects and visualizes anonymous love-related messages posted on the internet and presents them in “real-time” to the user on an interactive platform. The website interconnects various color-coded lines to represent different emotions and their correlation.




The Unsent Project (2015) by Rora Blue
The Unsent Project allows people to anonymously share their unsent messages to their loved ones, capturing raw and unexpressed human emotions. The website curates these personal messages and creates an archive highlighting the vulnerability of human beings.




We Feel Fine (2006) by Jonathan Harris and Sep Kamvar

We Feel Fine tracks and collects millions of blog posts that contain the words “I feel” to create a massive portrait of collective human emotion. The website combines these different quotes with images to showcase the disparity in human emotions, ranging from humorous to serious.
